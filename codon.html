Codon

What is a Codon?

A codon is a sequence of three nucleotides in messenger RNA (mRNA) that specifies an amino acid or a signal during protein synthesis. Codons are part of the genetic code, which cells use to translate genetic information into proteins.

Each codon acts like a three-letter "word" that tells the ribosome what to do during protein synthesis.

Structure

A codon consists of three RNA bases. The four RNA bases are:

  • Adenine (A)
  • Uracil (U)
  • Cytosine (C)
  • Guanine (G)

Examples of codons include:

  • AUG
  • GGU
  • UUU
  • CAG
