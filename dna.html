DNA

What is DNA?

DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) is the hereditary molecule that stores genetic information in almost all living organisms. It contains the instructions needed to build, maintain, and reproduce cells. DNA acts as a biological blueprint, directing the production of proteins that allow organisms to grow, function, and survive.

Nearly every cell in the human body contains DNA, and most of this DNA is found within the nucleus.

Structure

DNA consists of two long strands that twist around each other to form a double helix.

Each strand is made of repeating units called nucleotides. Every nucleotide contains:

  • A phosphate group
  • A deoxyribose sugar
  • A nitrogenous base

The four nitrogenous bases found in DNA are:

  • Adenine (A)
  • Thymine (T)
  • Cytosine (C)
  • Guanine (G)

These bases pair according to specific rules:

  • Adenine pairs with Thymine
  • Cytosine pairs with Guanine

The sequence of these bases stores genetic information.

How It Works

DNA stores information using the order of its nitrogenous bases. Cells can read this information and use it to produce proteins.

When a cell needs to make a protein:

  1. A gene is copied into messenger RNA (mRNA).
  2. The mRNA travels to a ribosome.
  3. The ribosome reads the mRNA in groups of three bases called codons.
  4. Codons specify amino acids.
  5. Amino acids are linked together to form a protein.

Functions

DNA performs several essential functions:

  • Stores genetic information
  • Provides instructions for protein synthesis
  • Passes hereditary traits from parents to offspring
  • Directs growth and development
  • Regulates cellular activities
  • Ensures genetic information is copied during cell division

Without DNA, cells would not know how to function properly.

Role in Genetics and Biology

DNA is the foundation of genetics because genes are made of DNA.

The sequence of DNA determines:

  • Which proteins are produced
  • How cells function
  • Many inherited traits
  • How organisms grow and develop

Mutations in DNA can sometimes alter proteins and lead to genetic variation or disease.

Human Relevance

Human DNA contains approximately:

  • 3 billion base pairs
  • About 20,000 protein-coding genes
  • 23 pairs of chromosomes

Scientists study DNA to understand:

  • Genetic diseases
  • Human ancestry
  • Evolution
  • Personalized medicine
  • Forensic identification

DNA technology is used in medicine, research, and criminal investigations.

Interesting Facts

  • DNA was first identified in 1869 by Friedrich Miescher.
  • The double-helix structure was described in 1953.
  • Nearly every cell in the human body contains the same DNA.
  • More than 99% of human DNA is shared among all people.
  • If stretched out, the DNA in a single human cell would be about 2 meters (6 feet) long.
  • The human genome contains about 3 billion DNA base pairs.

Key Takeaway

DNA is the molecule that stores and transmits genetic information. Through genes and protein synthesis, DNA controls the structure, function, growth, and reproduction of living organisms, making it one of the most important molecules in biology.
