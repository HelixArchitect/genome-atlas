DNA

What is DNA?

DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) is the hereditary molecule that stores genetic information in almost all living organisms. DNA contains the instructions needed to build, maintain, and reproduce cells.

Structure

DNA consists of two complementary strands that twist around each other to form a double helix. Each strand is made of nucleotides containing a sugar, phosphate group, and nitrogenous base.

Bases

  • Adenine (A)
  • Thymine (T)
  • Cytosine (C)
  • Guanine (G)

Adenine pairs with Thymine, and Cytosine pairs with Guanine.

Functions

  • Stores genetic information
  • Provides instructions for protein production
  • Passes hereditary information from parents to offspring
  • Guides growth, development, and cellular function

DNA to Protein

DNA contains genes that can be transcribed into mRNA. Ribosomes then read the mRNA and translate its codons into amino acids, producing proteins.

Interesting Facts

  • DNA was first identified in 1869 by Friedrich Miescher.
  • The double-helix structure was described in 1953.
  • Human DNA contains about 3 billion base pairs.
  • Nearly every cell in the human body contains DNA.
